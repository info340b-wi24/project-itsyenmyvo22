Project Proposal

Problem Description

ARMY is the name of the BTS fandom. It's one of the biggest fandoms in the world and is known for being a passionate, active, and organized unit. They are a group of diverse individuals from around the world, consisting of people from different cultures, languages, and age demographics. Since their community is so large, there is an overwhelming amount of content that is produced. ARMY can get lost in the abundance of platforms, resources, and outlets disguised as catering to them. Finding the correct and up-to-date schedule information, sharing interests, and connecting with ARMYs is a difficult task with how dispersed everything is on the internet.

The information technology problem within this space is that there is no centralized hub specifically tailored to ARMY that can allow for easy access to information, activities such as trading merchandise or game challenges, and creating a deeper connection within the community.

Previous attempts to solve the problem of having a centralized hub include platforms such as Weverse or official social media platforms like Twitter (X). As effective as these platforms are, they do not fully solve the problem that we are addressing. For example, Weverse is more catered towards purchasing merchandise and watching video content and falls flat when it comes to creating a connection within the community. Twitter (X) contains an overwhelming amount of information that is unorganized, making it difficult to find and filter the right information that someone needs. In addition, Twitter (X) can be a dangerous platform for younger audiences as the app does come with age restrictions that many users don't abide by.

App Description

Users of Application

The users of this application are people part of the BTS ARMY. While this website is open to all BTS ARMY members, it specifically caters to younger BTS fans who may be unable to access Twitter (X) due to age restrictions. This app will try to support young fans who want to support BTS but cannot connect with members on Twitter (X). In addition, this website can help users in rural areas, who struggle to form a community and may not have the means to attend a BTS concert to find a community, so this website will foster connections.

Interactive Features

  • A game for members
    • Users are given a 10-second clip of a BTS song (or a different length based on difficulty level), and the players guess the song title based on the audio clip.
    • The top contestants appear on a leaderboard.
  • A trading feature
    • Players upload their BTS trading card or BTS merchandise that they want to trade.
    • They can search and filter the desired trading card/merchandise, and offer to trade.
  • A calendar feature listing all the events in the BTS ARMY
    • These events can include dates for BTS concerts, fan meetings, and other significant events such as music release days.
    • Users can add events they plan to attend to their personal calendars and set reminders of upcoming events.

Users will have the ability to create and post their own trading cards which will then be made publicly available for viewing by all other users.

How the Web Application Solves the Problem

Using this app will help solve the problem of information overload within the BTS ARMY. Many members tweet events, but there is limited organization that can overwhelm users. The calendar feature solves this problem by providing an organized hub of events. In addition, this web application allows for a safe space to trade cards and play games. This means that children and younger BTS fans to connect and be a part of the BTS community, without having to rely on websites like Twitter, which is unsuitable for younger fans.
